1
Nĩ tũigaga nyamũ mĩciĩ-inĩ. Ici nĩ ta ng’ombe, ngũkũ,
ng’ondu, mbũri, Ndigiri, na mbaata.
We keep animals in our homes. These are cow, hen,
sheep, goat , donkey and duck.
2
Ng'ombe ĩtũheaga iria na nyama.
Mai na mathugumo ma ng’ombe mathondekaga thumu
mwega mũno. Thumu ũyũ nĩ wĩkĩragwo mĩmera
ĩkamera wega mũno.
A cow gives us milk.
Cow droppings and urine make good manure. This
manure makes crop very healthy.
3
Ngũkũ ĩtũheaga matumbĩ na nyama.
Ona no ĩtũheaga thumu.
A hen gives us eggs and meat.
It also gives us manure.
4
Mbaata ĩtũheaga matumbĩ na nyama.
Ona no ĩtũheaga thumu.
A duck gives us eggs and meat.
It also gives us manure.
5
Mbũri ĩtũheaga nyama na thumu. Mbũri imwe nĩ
itũheaga iria. Iria rĩa mbũri nĩ rĩega mũno rĩa kũruga
cai.
A goat gives us meat and manure. Some goats give
milk. The milk is very good for making tea.
6
Ng'ondu ĩtũheaga nyama na thumu. No ĩtũheaga
maguoya. Maguoya mathondekaga nguo.
A sheep gives us meat and manure. It also gives us
wool. Wool is used to make cloths.
7
Ndigiri ĩkuaga mĩrigo iitũ kuuma handũ kinya harĩa
hangĩ.
A donkey carries luggage from one place to another.
8
Ona no tũigaga nyau na ngui.
We also keep cats and dogs.
9
Nyau ĩtũteithagia kũnina mbĩa na ngaara mĩciĩ-inĩ.
A cat keeps rats and mice out of our homes.
10
Ngui ĩtũteithagia na ũgitĩri wa mĩciĩ.
Kwanyu mũigĩte nyamũ irĩkũ?
A dog keeps our home save.
Which animals do you keep at your home?
